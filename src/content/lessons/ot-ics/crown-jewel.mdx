## The wrong asset register breaks containment

Every IT team has an asset inventory. Most of them are organised by **what the
asset is** (hostname, OS, IP). That's not enough for ACDC's Step 2. The defender
needs to know **what would break if the asset stopped working**, **what depends
on it**, and **what an adversary would want from it**. Without those three
dimensions, you cannot make a safe containment call in Step 4.

The artifact that captures all three is a **Crown Jewel Register**.

## Three dimensions of "asset"

| Dimension | Question it answers |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Technical | What is it — hostname, OS, role, network position? |
| Business | What mission does it support? Who owns it? What's the SLA? |
| Adversarial | What would an attacker want from or do to it? |

The third dimension is the one most asset databases miss. It's also the one
that links your asset register to your hunt hypotheses and your detection rules.

## The toolkit script

```bash
# Add one asset (interactive — fill in everything you know)
./scripts/crown-jewel.sh add --engagement acme-q2 --name 'SCADA-MTU-01' \
  --owner 'OT Ops' \
  --purdue 2 \
  --criticality CRITICAL \
  --sector Electric \
  --deps 'AD,DNS,Historian-PI' \
  --threats 'Operate IED,Disable safety' \
  --attck 'T0855,T0858'

# Bulk import from a CSV or JSON
./scripts/crown-jewel.sh import --engagement acme-q2 --input assets.csv

# Render — sorted by criticality, with adversary-goal context inline
./scripts/crown-jewel.sh render --engagement acme-q2 --format md
./scripts/crown-jewel.sh render --engagement acme-q2 --format html
```

A finished register lives at
`~/assessments/<engagement>/02-active-recon/crown-jewels.json`. The same
script can write under `~/incidents/<id>/02-scope/` for IR engagements.

## Why "dependencies" is the most important field

If you contain SCADA-MTU-01 and your operator console depends on it, you've
just made the OT environment less safe than the intrusion was. **You cannot
defend what you do not understand the dependencies of.**

Walk the dependency chain in both directions:

- **What does this asset need to function?** (AD, DNS, NTP, historian, jump host)
- **What needs this asset to function?** (operator UI, alarm system, downstream PLCs)

Both lists get into the register. When Step 4 (containment) starts, the
process engineer reads the dependencies before any isolation action is approved.

## Why "adversary goals" sit alongside the asset

Hunt hypotheses in Step 1 are phrased as "if [adversary] is doing [TTP] in
our environment, we should see [observable] in [data source] at [logical
location]." The Crown Jewel register tells you the *logical location*. The
adversary-goals column tells you which TTPs are worth hunting against that
asset specifically.

For SCADA-MTU-01 (a Master Terminal Unit running Modbus to a fleet of PLCs):

- Adversary goal: "operate IEDs" → hunt for T0855 (Unauthorized Command Message), T0858 (Change Operating Mode)
- Adversary goal: "disable safety" → hunt for T0826 (Loss of Availability), T0816 (Device Restart/Shutdown)
- Adversary goal: "stage future attack" → hunt for T0846 (Remote System Discovery), T0852 (Screen Capture)

## How this feeds Step 4

Containment in OT environments uses the **three-stakeholder gate** (IR Lead +
Network Operations + Process Engineer). The process engineer's veto exists
precisely because they understand dependencies the IR team doesn't. A good
Crown Jewel register front-loads that conversation — the dependencies are
already documented, the adversary goals are already prioritised, and the
containment decision becomes "does this action break any documented
dependency?" rather than "let's call the plant manager and find out."

## How this feeds Step 1 next cycle

When the Course of Action matrix in Step 5 closes detection gaps, those new
detections should map to specific Crown Jewels — "we now detect T0858 against
PLC-Heater-Loop-A and PLC-Heater-Loop-B because of the FrostyGoop case."
That mapping informs Step 1 of the next cycle: detection coverage per crown
jewel becomes a measurable defensive metric.
